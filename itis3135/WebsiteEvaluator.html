Website Evaluations

Apple Inc.

The Apple Inc. website is a prime example of effective design principles.

  • Contrast: The website utilizes a clean and minimalistic design with a predominantly white background, providing excellent contrast for its content.
  • Repetition: Apple maintains a consistent design language and layout throughout its website, ensuring a cohesive and recognizable brand identity.
  • Alignment: Elements on the Apple website are meticulously aligned, creating a visually pleasing and organized layout.
  • Proximity: Apple groups related content together logically, allowing users to quickly access the information they need without unnecessary clutter or confusion.

The Apple website is an excellent example of a design that adheres to the CRAP principles, contributing to a user-friendly and visually appealing experience.

Craigslist

Craigslist is a classified advertisements website known for its outdated design and usability issues.

  • Contrast: The website lacks contrast with a plain and outdated color scheme, making it difficult to differentiate sections and text.
  • Repetition: There is minimal repetition of design elements, resulting in a lack of consistency across various pages and listings.
  • Alignment: Elements on Craigslist are often misaligned, with irregular spacing and unpredictable layouts, creating a disorganized appearance.
  • Proximity: Content is loosely organized, and it's often challenging to distinguish between different sections or categories of listings, leading to confusion.

Craigslist fails to follow the CRAP principles effectively, primarily due to its outdated design, lack of visual hierarchy, and poor usability, which results in a frustrating user experience.
